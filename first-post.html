The many faces of Monte Carlo Techniques¶

Introduction¶

The use of Monte Carlo (MC) technique to predict stock prices is illustrated in this article. The application of this technique is very broad as such perhaps there are only handfull simulation studies which do not overlap with the principles of this technique. In finance, MC is used among others by :

  • Portfolio managers to assess the variability of their asset allocation strategy
  • Risk Manager to asses the tail loss in case of extreme events happen like the financial crisis in 2007 or the burst of internet bubble in 1998

  • Trader to price an over-the-counter (OTC) trade with other conterparties

    In data science, some of typical uses of MC are among others to:

  • Find optimal feature combination within feature space for feature selection

Estimating the value of Pi with Monte Carlo¶

Estimating the value of Pi is another example of the application MC method to a toy problem which do not necessary have meaningful real life applications but serves as an illustration of the versatility of the MC method.

Suppose that you randomly shoot at circle-shape dart board with diameter of 1 located inside an unit square. The probability of the dart board being hit is the ratio of the area of the circle to the area of the unit square $A$ where

$$A=\pi r^2 = \pi (0.5)^2 = 0.25 \pi$$

if $X$ is an indicator random variable which takes value 1 if the dart hits the target and 0 otherwise, then

$$ A = P(X=1) = 0 . P(X=0) + 1. P(X=1) = E(X) = \frac{X_1+X_2+...+X_n}{n} $$

The approximate value of $A$ is then the average number of darts which hit the target. This simple counting to calculate the value of $A$ can be done easily in python. Once $A$ is obtained, $\pi$ can be derived as $\pi = 4A$.
